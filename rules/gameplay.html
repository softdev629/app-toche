Gameplay & Points

Toche is a 1-on-1 sport.

It consists of throwing four balls at a target to get points.

You and your opponent take turns to throw the balls, and you have to run like crazy the whole match!

It's pretty simple, really. You don’t need to be a genius to play Toche, but you'll become one.

RIGHT & LEFT

Each player has four balls.


In each round, you first throw two balls with your right hand, and then two with your left. Keeping this order is mandatory, you will get penalised otherwise. You can only hold one ball with the hand you’re throwing.

SCORING SYSTEM

You throw the balls from the platform to the target and add the points that you make every time you throw. If the ball goes inside the target, you get 2 points. If the ball touches the rim, and bounces outside the target base, you get 1 point. If the ball touches the rim, bounces on the base, and lands outside the base, you get 1 point. If the ball miraculously lands on the rim and stays there, you get 1 point—even if the next ball hits the target and makes it fall inside. If the ball touches the rim, and directly lands inside the base, you get nothing. If the ball touches the rim, bounces on the base, and lands inside the base, you get nothing.

COUNTING

After throwing each ball, you must add (in your mind) the points you just made to the points you had, and then say out loud the new total amount. This procedure enhances your concentration power and reduces the risk of misunderstandings and cheating. The two players get to decide on the language they want to count in, which should be the same for both players. English should be the common ground for all players in case they speak different native languages.

TAKING TURNS

In Toche, players take turns to throw, run, pick up, run, cheer and run. That is all you’re going to be doing for an hour, but, oh boy, if it’s fun! And tiring. Running means running, not jogging or walking like you’re holding a number two. Not running is a major violation of the game and is punished with pain and penalties. More on this in a bit. If you leave a ball behind, you must run and throw only three balls in your next turn. If your opponent touches your ball, he will be penalised. You can’t throw before you get to the platform. Once you arrive at the platform, you can throw. You don’t need to wait for your friend to move out. More on this exquisite aspect of the game in the burning section.

Throw your balls: two with the right, then two with the left. And don't forget to count.

Run to the target.

Pick up your balls and be quick about it because your ass is exposed!

Run to the cheering area to be annoying.

Cheer for yourself if you get approval from your lungs. Otherwise, just breathe.

Run back to the platform. if you are lucky, you can burn your friend.

BURNING

If you arrive to the target and your opponent is still picking her balls, you can throw and burn her. Burning gives you 2 points! Once you burn her once, stop throwing and enjoy the view: your opponent has to pick up your balls and bring them to you. You will throw again your four balls.


Burning gives you the opportunity to make 10 points in one turn!


When delivering the balls, your opponent must run—or it will be another penalty. She must put the balls on your hands, gently. Smiling at this point would rub it in, so it’s highly recommended.


BURNING PROTECTION.

You can’t burn your opponent if she is standing to the left of the barrier. Even if you are just using a cone, the position of the opponent relevant to the cone is enough to decide if she’s standing behind the barrier or not. If it’s a close call, then it won’t count as a penalty. On the bright side, you still got to burn her!


When you are picking up the balls, you can of course try to dodge the ball. However, make sure you still run because if you stay near the target too long, you could arrive late to the Cheering Area and still be penalised (more on that on the Penalties Section)

CHEERING

In Toche you are allowed to cheer for yourself, sing, dance, do the wave, joke and, in general, do anything that can distract your opponent. However, don’t step out of the Cheering Area.


Rationale: Since Toche is also a sport of the mind, cheering is allowed because it makes you concentrate on your turn disregarding of the external stimuli produced by an obnoxious opponent. Professional football players need to deal with the pressure from 100,000 screaming spectators. In Toche, the pressure comes from your opponent trying to make you lose your concentration.


Warning: The downside of cheering is that it will make you lose your own concentration and, most important, it drains your energy. Use it wisely.
