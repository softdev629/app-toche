Glossary




Balls

The small balls you throw and then fetch.

Barrier

A net or wall placed to the side of the target, just outside the runway, to protect the players from getting burned. In its absence, a cone would do.

Burn

Or burning. When you hit your opponent because she didn’t pick up the balls fast enough. Arguably, the highlight of the sport.

Cheer

Or cheering. To be a clown in order to make your opponent loose his concentration. To cheer for yourself in order to energise you. Jumping, shouting, clapping, whistling, dancing and whatever comes to your mind that might get you in the zone, or get your opponent out of his.

Cheering Area

The area of the pitch where you stand and try to make your opponent loose his/her concentration. Alternatively, you can catch your breath.

Efficiency Percentage

or Efficiency % or E%. The percentage of points you make in relation to the maximum potential points in a match (approx. 1020 per match or 68 per song).

Match

A match lasts one hour. It’s made up of 5 sets, each comprising 5 songs, for a total of 25 songs. The actual running time is 50 minutes.

Platform

The area where the players stand when they throw. It is marked by a line.

Runway

The path where you run from the platform to the target.

Points

The unit for keeping track of the score during a song.

Sets

A group of five songs played continuously; it’s followed by a 1-minute break.

Songs

The unit for keeping the score during a match, it is also a unit of time.

Swing

Or swinging. To swing your arm more than one time when your throw.

Target

Where the balls should go in. Consists of the basket, pole and base.

Tie-break

When a song ends and both players have the same number of points, there is a tie-break. At the end of the match, they throw a round of penalties (or as many rounds as it takes) until there is a winner. All other rules apply during the tie-break.

Wall

An optional wall that goes behind the target and serves to stop the balls from rolling too far.
